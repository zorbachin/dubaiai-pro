FAQ

Everything You Want to Know

A standard 2-car garage (approximately 480 sq ft) typically runs $1,800–$2,400 with our epoxy flake system. Polyaspartic runs $2,400–$3,360 for the same space. Metallic epoxy starts around $3,360. Prices include diamond grinding, crack repair, base coat, full-broadcast flake, and UV-stable top coat. Use our calculator above for a personalized range.
Properly installed epoxy with a polyaspartic top coat should last 15–20+ years with normal residential use. The key is surface prep — floors that fail early almost always failed because the installer skipped proper concrete profiling (grinding). Our floors are backed by a lifetime warranty because we don't cut corners on prep.
Epoxy is the base coat and color system — it provides thickness and durability. Polyaspartic is a top coat that cures faster, handles UV better (no yellowing), and resists Austin's temperature swings better than epoxy alone. We use epoxy for the base and polyaspartic as the top coat on most jobs. A "full polyaspartic" system means both coats are polyaspartic — faster cure, higher cost, better for extreme heat exposure.
You can walk on the floor in 6–8 hours. You can park a car on it in 24 hours. Full cure (for heavy equipment or chemical exposure) takes 72 hours. We'll give you specific timing based on your coating system and the weather on install day.
Yes. Crack chasing and polyurea crack filler is included on every job. Oil stains are addressed during our diamond grinding process — mechanical profiling removes the contaminated surface layer that would prevent adhesion. Severe staining or damage may require an extra prep step, which we'll flag during the estimate with no surprises.
Yes — we serve all of Central Texas including Cedar Park, Round Rock, Pflugerville, Georgetown, Kyle, Buda, Westlake Hills, Lakeway, Leander, Bee Cave, and Manor. Call or submit a quote request and we'll confirm your address is in our service area.
Our warranty covers delamination, peeling, and flaking for the lifetime of the original installation. "Transferable" means it transfers to the new owner if you sell your home — which is a real selling point. The warranty doesn't cover damage from sharp objects, chemical spills left unattended, or improper cleaning products. We give you a full care guide at project completion.
Standard flake finish has a natural texture that provides good traction when wet. We also offer an anti-slip additive mixed into the top coat — a fine aluminum oxide that's invisible to the eye but adds significant grip. We recommend the anti-slip additive for exterior applications, pool decks, and garages used as workshops. Standard interior garages are typically fine without it.
