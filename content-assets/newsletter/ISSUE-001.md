## The First Call

I come home engaged with no money and no job. We are barely making rent in a sublet, the kind of broke where you do the math on groceries before you put them in the basket. We get married anyway. You don't wait around for perfect.

Then the war starts. Days after the wedding.

The sirens go, and we go down to the shelter. That becomes the rhythm for a while. And somewhere in the middle of it, something strange happens. My YouTube feed changes. The news just disappears. In its place there is nothing but people building things with AI. Build after build after build.

I can't look away.

I have no idea where any of it leads. But I start paying for the subscriptions anyway, one after another, just to test them with my own hands. I figure if even one of these tools helps me land a single job, it pays for itself. So I am up at strange hours, in a country at war, in a sublet I can barely afford, teaching myself what these things can actually do.

Midway through the war, deep in it, not knowing what I am doing or where it goes, we find out the baby is coming.

That changes the math. The obsession has to become something real.

So I make a call. I offer to build something real for someone's business.

Then a second call.

And then the hardest thing of all. I post about it. Out loud, with my name on it. That takes longer than any of the technical work, because posting was never about whether I could do the thing. It is the quiet fear that someone I know will see it and decide who I am.

I get over it. Barely. One post at a time.

Here is what it taught me, and it is the whole reason this exists.

AI is not magic. It is a tool that does the boring work that eats your hours, so you can have them back. That is the whole thing. And the longer life stayed hard, the clearer the mission got. I am building a business to take back my own time, and to give time back to other people.

Not to get anyone obsessed with AI. I had the obsession, and the obsession was never the point. I care about people who want to grow, who will pick up a good tool and actually use it well. I love solving problems. I do it on set every day, and this is the same instinct pointed at your business.

So this is me. Building it live. Exposed. No polished expert act, just the real thing in real time, mistakes and all.

Follow along. And whatever your version of the first call is, the first post, the first time you let the tool do the work instead of you, go and make it. I will be a few steps ahead, waving you on.

Zorba
