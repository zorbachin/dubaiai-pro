## The First Call

A little while ago, I had nothing that looked like a plan.

I came back home engaged — no money, no job, barely making rent in a sublet. The kind of broke where you do math on groceries. We got married anyway. Started building a life. Found out the family was going to grow.

Then the war came. Days after the wedding.

So there I was — newly married, a baby on the way, sitting in a bomb shelter when the sirens went off. And in that shelter, while everything outside felt uncertain, I got obsessed with one thing. Not the hype around AI. The *use* of it. What it could actually do. How much time it could hand back to a person who didn't have any to spare.

I read everything. I built little things. A thousand hours, give or take — most of them at strange hours.

Then I did the hard part. I made a call. Offered to build something real for someone's business.

Then a second call.

And then the hardest one — I posted about it. Out loud. With my name on it. That took longer than any of the technical stuff. Because posting was never about ability. It's the quiet fear that someone you know will see it and judge.

I got over it. Barely. One post at a time.

Here's what I learned, and it's the whole reason this exists:

**AI isn't magic. It's a tool that does the boring, time-eating work so you get your hours back.** That's it. And the mission only got clearer the harder things got: I'm building a business to take back my own time — and to give time back to other people.

Not to get anyone "obsessed with AI." I don't care about that. I care about people who want to grow, who'll pick up a good tool and use it well. I love solving problems — I do it on set every day, and this is the same instinct pointed at your business.

So this is me. Building live. Exposed. No polished expert act — the real thing, in real time, mistakes and all.

Follow along. And whatever your version of it is — the first call, the first post, the first time you let the tool do the work instead of you — go make it. I'll be a few steps ahead, waving you on.

— Zorba
*Let AI Do It · letaidoit.pro*
