## The First Call

It is pretty romantic, if I am honest. Getting engaged at the Duomo at sunset, both of us in clothes we had just bought, the labels still reading "Made in Milan." We had been traveling for six weeks. The ups, the downs, the small adventures you only get when you travel on a budget. And at the very least, she was surprised.

I had the perfect alibi to film, too. I had just bought a new iPhone microphone, and obviously I needed to test it. It was also our last night in Italy, our last night before going home, which meant we were dressed nice anyway. So no one thought twice about the little bag I was carrying. A tripod, the microphones, and the ring box she somehow did not notice.

[PHOTO: the engagement at the Duomo, golden hour]

It was hot that day. Italy in August is hot. We kept circling the piazza, waiting, because it had to be golden hour. It had to be. We started an awkward fake interview about the trip, the highlights, how it all felt, and by then we were both tired. Then she wanted to switch places and ask me the questions. So it had to be then.

It is awkward. We are not the big public display kind of people. But what a story. What a picture.

That is the definition of Instagram versus reality.

Because here is the reality. We are standing there in fresh Italian clothes, at the Duomo, at sunset, getting engaged, and I do not have a job.

When I moved to Israel a year before that, my company laid me off two days after I booked my ticket. Which turned out fine, because the company went under six weeks later. But my six figure cushion went with it. My determination, though, was still very much intact.

Within six weeks of landing, Meytal and I met. Hours before a flight I had, of course. When I came back we kept seeing each other, and not long after, a rocket hit her building and jammed her door shut. She was locked out of her own apartment. So she moved into my room, a tiny second bedroom in a shared place. On Yom Haatzmaut, Independence Day, we moved into our first sublet. We would end up extending it twice.

Then the travel. Then the Duomo. Then home, with no idea where the money was going to come from.

Sometimes angels sent it. One in particular, almost like he was quietly tracking my bank account, showing up right when it got thin.

As the wedding got closer, the freelance gig I was living on was clearly ending, and I needed to find something real.

I should pause here and say something, because it matters. In that same stretch I got a film into active development, with an investor signed on. It is in the works and I am genuinely thrilled about it. I also have a TV series being pitched around to some big names, the kind of thing that would change my life. But even when those land, they land months from now. And our safety net was never that safe.

We get to the wedding, and it is amazing. People flew in from around the world. The culture collisions in that room, Alaskan and Ethiopian, Israeli and American, all at once, in a way nobody there had quite seen before. It was something.

[PHOTO: the wedding, the cultures colliding]

But the reality after the music stops is that you have to pay for it. The wedding, the rent, the life. And not nine days later, the war began. Movement got restricted. Meytal was off work.

Thankfully we have a mamad, a safe room. So we spent the first month of our marriage finally in our own place, after eight months of subletting, and then we were in the spare room. Thankfully my mom had us set up a bed in there for when she visited, so we did not really suffer for it.

But our place is small. It is not a place to raise a family. And this is where the house comes in.

Family friends of ours built a beautiful home, not far from my mother in law, near Netanya. For their own reasons, they cannot live in it. Which means we have a real chance at our dream home. Which means I have a mission. I want to support my family, get us into that home, and use AI to do it.

On Passover, we went around the table and each said what makes us feel free. Without thinking, I said AI. And then I explained why. It gives me the ability to create my ideas and actually bring them to life. That has always been the hard part for a filmmaker without infinite money sitting at his fingertips. It is the same reason I love standup. You find out fast if it works. AI is like that. You see the result quickly.

The film will come. The series might. But I was done letting my ideas sit and wait on other people's money and other people's yeses. AI did not make me wait.

So I started building a site for myself, zorbot.io, trying to pull my scattered work and passions into one thing people could actually grab onto. I built a chatbot trained on my own ChatGPT history, my book on how to think, all of it. And I realized, that is a product. That is how bloop was born.

Then my algorithm shifted. I stopped hearing about the news, or the war, or anything else. It was all how to wire one new tool into another, Hermes into Claude, names I had never heard of popping up everywhere. So I tinkered. Then I subscribed. Then I subscribed again, and again, probably a thousand dollars and well over a thousand hours, just building and exploring.

I was not new to AI. But I was new to this. And this new part is the part I love. It creates real opportunities to get your time back.

That is when I started reaching out to businesses. That is when the mission, the goal, and the plan finally came together.

I built buildyourbot.io. I built selfsellingai.pro. Call agents, chatbots, inventory automation, and a lot more.

I think a lot about the people who carried us when things were thin. The angel who always seemed to show up at the exact right moment, right when the account got low. Part of why I am building this is so that one day I get to be that for someone else.

And now this is what I do. The focus is on you, so you never have to learn everything I learned to get back the most valuable thing there is. Your time.

That is what I am here for. So reach out. Tell me what is taking your time away, and we will see if I can get it back.

As for me, I am doing this out loud, in public, building toward a home for my family one call at a time. The Duomo was the easy yes. This is the harder one, and I am making it anyway. Come along, and go make your own first call while you are at it.
