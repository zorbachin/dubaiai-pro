## The First Call

It is pretty romantic, if I am honest.

The Duomo at sunset. Both of us in clothes we had bought that week, labels still reading "Made in Milan." Six weeks of travel behind us, all the ups and downs and small adventures you only get on a budget. And, at the very least, she was surprised.

I had the perfect cover to film it, too. I had just bought a new iPhone microphone, and obviously I needed to test it. It was also our last night in Italy, our last night before home, so we were dressed up anyway. Nobody thought twice about the little bag I was carrying. A tripod. The microphones. And a ring box she somehow did not notice.

(The ring inside it came from Amazon. That is a story for another issue, and it gets better.)

[PHOTO: the engagement at the Duomo, golden hour]

It was hot. Italy in August always is. We kept circling the piazza, waiting, because it had to be golden hour. It had to be. So I started an awkward fake interview about the trip. The highlights. How it all felt. We were both tired. And then she said she wanted to switch places and interview me.

So it had to be then.

We are not the grand public gesture kind of people. But what a story. What a picture.

Which is the entire definition of Instagram versus reality.

Because here is the reality behind that photo. Fresh Italian clothes, the Duomo, the sunset, the proposal. And I did not have a job.

When I moved to Israel the year before, my company laid me off two days after I booked the ticket. It worked out, in a dark way, because the company folded six weeks later anyway. My six figure cushion folded with it. My determination did not.

Six weeks after I landed, I met Meytal. Hours before a flight, of course. I came back, we kept seeing each other, and then a rocket hit her building and jammed her door shut. Locked out of her own apartment, she moved into my room, a tiny second bedroom in a shared flat. On Yom Haatzmaut, Independence Day, we got our first sublet. We would extend it twice.

Then the trip. Then the Duomo. Then home, with no idea where the money would come from.

Sometimes it just arrived. Angels. One in particular, who showed up right as the account ran thin, almost like he was watching the balance.

By the time the wedding got close, the freelance work keeping us afloat was drying up. I needed something real.

Now, I should pause, because this next part is true and it matters. In that same stretch I got a film into active development, with an investor attached. It is happening, and I cannot wait. I also have a TV series being pitched to names that could change my life. But those land months from now, if they land. And our safety net was never that safe.

The wedding itself was something else. People came from around the world. Alaskan and Ethiopian, Israeli and American, all colliding in one room in a way nobody there had quite seen before.

[PHOTO: the wedding — the cultures colliding. Use the 3 you sent: (1) the whole room around you under the spotlight mid-celebration, (2) the chuppah/ceremony with the green dress and the kippot, (3) the first dance on the lit floor.]

And then the music stops, and somebody has to pay for it. The wedding. The rent. The life. Nine days later, the war started. Movement closed in. Meytal was out of work.

We have a mamad, a safe room. So the first month of our marriage, our first month finally in our own place after eight months of sublets, we spent partly in that spare room. My mom had set up a bed in there for her visits, so honestly, we were fine.

But the place is small. It is not where you raise a family. Which brings me to the house.

Family friends built a beautiful home near Netanya, close to my mother in law. For their own reasons, they cannot live in it. Which means we have a real shot at our dream home. Which means I have a mission. Support my family. Get us into that house. Use AI to do it.

Then came Passover. We went around the table naming what makes us feel free. Without thinking, I said AI. And I meant it. It lets me create my ideas and actually bring them to life, which has always been the hard part for a filmmaker without a fortune at his fingertips. It is why I love standup, too. You know within seconds if it worked. AI is like that. The result comes fast.

The film will come. The series might. But I was done letting my ideas wait on other people's money and other people's yeses. AI does not make me wait.

So I built a site for myself, zorbot.io, trying to gather my scattered work and obsessions into one thing people could actually grab onto. I trained a chatbot on my own ChatGPT history, my book on how to think, all of it. And it hit me. That is a product. That is how bloop was born.

Then my feed flipped. No more news, no more war, nothing but people wiring one tool into another. Hermes into Claude. Names I had never heard of, appearing by the hour. So I tinkered. Then I subscribed. Then I subscribed again, and again. A thousand dollars and well over a thousand hours, just building and seeing what was actually possible.

I was not new to AI. I was new to this. And this is the part I love. It hands real time back to real people.

That is when I started reaching out to businesses. That is when the mission and the plan finally clicked into one thing. I built buildyourbot.io. I built selfsellingai.pro. Call agents, chatbots, inventory automation, and a lot more.

I still think about the people who carried us when it was thin. That one angel, every time, right on cue. A lot of why I am building this is so that one day I get to be that for someone else.

So this is what I do now, and the whole point of it is you. You should not have to spend what I spent, in money or hours or sleepless nights, to get back the most valuable thing there is. Your time.

So tell me what is stealing yours, and we will see if I can get it back.

As for me, I am doing this in the open. Building toward a home for my family, one call at a time. The Duomo was the easy yes. This one is harder, and I am saying it out loud anyway. Come along. And while you are here, go make your own first call.

Zorba
Let AI Do It · letaidoit.pro
