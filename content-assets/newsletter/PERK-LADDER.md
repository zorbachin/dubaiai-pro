toward the real goal: products sold and clients booked. Substack is the hub that

## The ladder

**1. Reader (free, on the web)**
- Reads the free story issues. No perks yet. The goal is to convert them to a subscriber.

**2. Free subscriber (email)**
- Every story issue of Zorbasphere, in their inbox.
- **Welcome perk: 15% off the Etsy shop** (code `DISPATCH15`).
- An occasional free guide.
- The Build Log updates (if you run it here too).

**3. Paid subscriber (suggested $7/mo or $70/yr, with a "Founding" tier higher)**
- Everything above, plus:
- **40% off the Etsy shop** (private code, e.g. `INSIDER40`).
- **One free guide every month** (rotate through the 20).
- **Early access** to every new product, tool, and demo before it goes public.
- **Behind-the-scenes build breakdowns** (how the closing tool, the bots, etc. were actually made).
- **First dibs and a discount on a consulting audit call** with you.
- The full prompt vault.

**4. Client (the real revenue)**
- The people who reply, book a call, and hire you. The whole ladder is built to funnel here. Every guide ends with a "work with me" page; every paid perk warms them up.

## The Etsy discount mechanics
Etsy supports shop coupon codes (Shop Manager → Marketing → Sales & discounts → Create code).
- Create **`DISPATCH15`** = 15% off, shared only with free subscribers.
- Create **`INSIDER40`** = 40% off, shared only inside the paid posts.
- Never post the codes publicly, so the perk stays exclusive to subscribers.

_I draft the codes and the copy; you create the two codes in Etsy in about two minutes._
