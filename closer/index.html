The Problem

Contractors Leave Money on the Table Every Day

The #1 reason a homeowner doesn't sign at the estimate? "I need to think about it." That almost always means: I can't visualize it, I'm unsure about the price, and there's no urgency to decide now. Previz fixes all three at once.

🖼️

No visualization = no emotional buy-in

Customers can't commit to a $2,400 floor they can only imagine. A photorealistic render of their actual garage — taken 60 seconds ago — makes it real.

💬

Verbal quotes are forgettable

Reps recite prices, homeowners forget them. A branded proposal they can forward to a spouse — with tiers and a deposit button — keeps you in the conversation.

⏰

No urgency = ghosted follow-ups

Without a deposit taken on the spot, your slot evaporates. The proposal's "secure your install date" framing creates a reason to act now.

"We closed maybe 40% of our estimates before. Now it's closer to 65%. The difference is the render — when they see their actual garage with the metallic copper floor, most people just say 'yeah, let's do it.' The proposal with the deposit button does the rest."

Why this is fast to build

The hardest parts of a SaaS backend — AI rendering, payment processing, email, auth, database — already exist and run in production at zorbot.io. The _core modules (imageGeneration, stripe, notification, storage, trpc, drizzle) are designed to be copied into a new product. That's how TrailersMade was built in days, not months.

Phase 2 is mostly schema + plumbing. Phase 3 adds a tenant admin UI. The genuinely hard engineering — the AI render quality, the proposal UX, the pricing engine — is already done in the Phase 1 prototype. We're not rebuilding from scratch; we're promoting working demo code to a production backend.

The LiDAR measurement in Phase 3 is the only real new frontier, and the spec deliberately de-risks it: build it as a throwaway spike in Phase 1/2 to confirm feasibility before it gates the platform.
