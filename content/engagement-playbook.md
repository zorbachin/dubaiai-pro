# Engagement: grow on purpose, not on hope

> Your question: "find more ways to increase engagement, or just keep posting for a month and see if it's growing?"
> Honest answer: **do both — but 'just post for a month' only works if the posts are built to compound.** Here's the system so the month actually means something.

---

## The one truth
Consistency is necessary but not sufficient. Posting daily for 30 days with no intent gets you 30 posts and a flat line. Posting daily *with a few high-leverage habits* is how it snowballs. Same effort, very different month.

## The 5 levers that actually move it (in priority order)

**1. The hook is 80% of the post.**
First line / first slide / first 2 seconds decide everything. If it doesn't make someone stop, nothing else matters. Steal structures: "Stop doing X." / "Nobody tells you Y." / "I did X so you don't have to." / a number ("3 prompts that…"). You already write great ones — front-load them, every time.

**2. Pick ONE platform to win first.**
Yours is **Facebook** (your most fertile ground). Go deep there, cross-post everywhere else. Trying to win 4 platforms at once = winning none. Win FB, repurpose to IG/LI/X/Substack.

**3. Engage outward 15 min/day — this is the cheat code.**
Reach isn't only about *posting*; it's about *showing up in other people's comments.* Leave 5–10 thoughtful comments a day on bigger accounts in your niche (small business, AI-for-real-people, founders). You borrow their audience. This single habit outperforms most posting tweaks.

**4. One idea → five posts (the flywheel).**
Don't invent content daily — *repurpose.* One idea = a Substack note, a carousel, a 30-sec video, a long post, an article section. The "vending machine" carousel and the "I sell time" article are the *same idea* in different clothes. That's the move: mine each idea fully.

**5. End with a reason to engage.**
"Save this." "Reply with the task you dread." "Restack if this hit." Engagement you *ask for* is engagement you *get* — and replies/DMs are where leads actually come from. A like is vanity; a reply is a conversation.

## Measure the right thing (not likes)
For 30 days, track weekly — only 4 numbers:
- **Saves + shares** (did it compound?)
- **Profile visits** (did it make people curious about *you*?)
- **Replies + DMs** (did it start conversations?)
- **Leads / "can you help me with…"** (the only number that pays rent)

Likes are weather. These four are climate.

## The 30-day plan (realistic, your stack already supports it)
- **Cadence:** 1 substantial post + 1 light note per day. Your nudge engine (Google Calendar) already schedules and reminds you — keep using it.
- **Weekly mix:** 2 value/teaching (carousels — saves), 2 story/build-in-public (you — trust), 1 short video (reach), daily light note (presence).
- **Daily 15 min** of outward commenting (lever #3). Non-negotiable.
- **Friday 10-min review:** the 4 numbers above. Double down on whatever format spiked.

## So: post for a month — yes. But this kind of month.
Don't "post and see." Post **with intent → repurpose → engage outward → ask for the action → review weekly.** If after 30 honest days the four numbers are flat, *then* we change the strategy with real data, not vibes. My bet: they won't be flat.
