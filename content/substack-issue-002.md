# I Don't Sell AI. I Sell Time.

*Everyone is selling AI right now. I'm selling the one thing it actually gives back to you.*

**Tags:** AI, building in public, productivity, founder, time management, honest marketing
**Audience:** Everyone

---

This week I landed my first paying client.

I keep waiting to feel like a fraud about it, because here's the thing nobody in my industry wants to admit: **they didn't care about AI.** Not even a little.

They're a family-run shop — two stores — and they've been quietly bleeding money for years over something stupid and human. The two stores can't see each other's inventory. So a sweatshirt sells out in one shop while a stack of the same sweatshirt sits collecting dust in the other. Customers order things online that aren't actually on the shelf, and someone has to make the awkward "sorry, we're out" phone call. Hours every week disappear into counting by hand.

I didn't walk in and say "let me show you the power of artificial intelligence." I said: *I can give you back about six hours a week and stop the sales that are walking out your door.*

That's it. That's the whole pitch. And it worked, because it was true.

## The thing everyone's selling, and the thing people actually want

Open any feed right now and it's a wall of the same word. AI this, AI agents, AI everything. Founders bolting "AI" onto the front of their company name like a turbocharger. And look — I get it. I'm obsessed with this stuff. I fell down the rabbit hole during a year of my life I'll tell you about another time (engaged at the Duomo, no job, savings gone, then a war and a shelter, and somewhere in the dark an obsession that turned into a business).

But here's what a year of building taught me: **nobody wakes up wanting AI.** They wake up wanting their Saturday. They want to not dread Monday's payroll. They want to leave the shop at 6 instead of 8. They want to coach their kid's game without their phone buzzing.

AI is just the most powerful tool we've ever had for buying that time back. That's all it is. A really, really good tool for doing the boring work that eats your hours — so you get the hours back.

## You can make more money. You can't make more Tuesday.

I think about this constantly. You can always make more money. You can borrow it, earn it, raise it. You cannot make more time. Nobody gets a single extra Tuesday.

So when I look at a business, I don't ask "where can we put AI?" I ask "what's the thing the owner secretly hates doing — and how do we make it just… happen?"

For the shop, it was inventory. For the next client it might be quotes, or payroll, or the email thread that's been open in their head for three days. The technology changes. The question never does.

## Building this in public

I'm doing all of this out loud — the wins, the awkward parts, the stuff that breaks. Partly because I think it's more honest, and partly because the hardest part of building was never the building. It was hitting *publish.* The quiet fear that someone you know will see it and decide who you are.

So I'm posting anyway. First client down. A long way to go.

If you run something, here's the only question worth asking yourself today: **what do you do every week that you'd pay almost anything to never do again?**

That's the thing. That's where the time is hiding.

I'll show you how I'd get it back.

*— Zorba*
