Quiet milestone today: first paying client for the thing I've been building.

Here's what's funny — they didn't care about "AI." Not one bit.

What they cared about: their two stores can't see each other's inventory. So something sells out in one shop while it's collecting dust in the other. Customers order things online that aren't actually on the shelf. Hours every week lost to counting by hand. Sales quietly walking out the door.

So I built them something boring and beautiful: one screen that shows what's where, flags the costly mistakes before they happen, and tells the team exactly what to move — on their phone, on the floor. It can only *read* their system, so it can't break anything that already works.

I keep saying it: I'm not selling AI. I'm selling time. The tech is just how we get there.
