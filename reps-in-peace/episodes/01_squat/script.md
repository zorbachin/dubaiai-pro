## 🪝 RE-HOOK #1 (0:30)

**[Coach kicks the chalk-dusted platform. Dust explodes into a shaft of overhead light.]**

**COACH:** Before you load the bar. Before you even *think* about loading the bar. There is one cue. Skip it and the next four minutes will not save you. I'm dead. I have time. Do you?

---

## 📋 SETUP (0:37–1:30)

**COACH:** Goblet squat first. Always. Before you back-squat anything, you earn it with a goblet. Hold a kettlebell at your chest. Elbows tucked. Feet shoulder-width. Toes flared fifteen degrees. Not thirty. You are not auditioning for the Bolshoi.

**BONESY:** What's a Bolshoi?

**COACH:** A ballet company. Point your toes less.

**BONESY:** Hands?

**COACH:** Cup the horns of the bell. Squeeze. Tension is universal. Grip the bell → glutes → lats → cross-tension through the spine. You are a coiled spring. *Hardstyle, or no style.*

**BONESY:** Okay. Hardstyle. Got it. What about my back?

**COACH:** Stacked. Neutral spine. Chest tall. Take a breath through your belt. Breathing behind the shield. Hold it.

---

## 🪝 RE-HOOK #2 (1:35)

**[Coach leans into camera. One eye socket lit crimson. Almost a whisper.]**

**COACH:** Here is the truth no algorithm rewards. Your squat does not stall because your legs are weak. It stalls because your *intent* is weak. You descend like you're sitting in a chair. Don't. Descend like you're crushing the floor on the way down. Watch.

---

## 🦴 THE LIFT (1:40–2:30)

**COACH:** Three seconds down. Hips back first — close that car door with your spine. Then knees track over toes. Sit *between* your heels. Not behind them. Not in front of them. Between.

**BONESY:** *(strained)* Something cracked.

**COACH:** That was the ego. Keep going. Hip crease below the kneecap. Anything shallower is a quarter squat, and a quarter squat is just an expensive way to stand up.

**[CUT: anatomical X-ray view of the knee tracking over the toe.]**

**COACH:** *(voiceover, calm)* Drive your knees *out* as you descend. Not forward. *Out.* You are spreading the floor apart with the outside edges of your feet. This is the cue. Do it and the squat fixes itself. Schoenfeld 2010 on knee valgus and force production — read it or don't, the bar doesn't care.

---

## 🪝 RE-HOOK #3 (2:35)

**COACH:** Now. Programming. This is where most lifters waste two years. Pay attention.

---

## 📊 SETS, REPS, PROGRAMMING (2:40–3:40)

**COACH:** Strength. Four to six sets. Three to five reps. Two to three minutes rest. Heavy load. Sub-maximal. Stop one rep before you want to. Strength is a skill, not a grind. You don't grind the piano. You practice it.

**BONESY:** And for size?

**COACH:** Hypertrophy. Three to four sets. Eight to twelve reps. Sixty to ninety seconds rest. Moderate load. The muscle should *cry*. The literature is settled — Schoenfeld, Krieger, every PhD with a barbell — moderate-load, moderate-rep maximizes growth per unit of effort.

**BONESY:** What if I want to squat more often?

**COACH:** Frequency. Twice, three times a week. Three working sets per session. Lower load. Grease the groove. The squat is technique-heavy. Frequency wins for technique-heavy lifts. The body learns through repetition, not violence.

**BONESY:** And volume?

**COACH:** Volume day. Once a week. Five to six working sets. Eat the suffering in one sitting. Only for the advanced. For everyone else: frequency.

**BONESY:** Which do I pick?

**COACH:** Whichever one you'll still be doing in sixteen weeks. Consistency beats theory. Theory is for skeletons.

**BONESY:** ...you are a skeleton.

**COACH:** *(beat)* I know.

---

## 🌀 LEIJA FLOW VARIATION (3:40–4:10)

**COACH:** One more thing. The flow. Once your goblet is honest, you earn this: goblet squat into a kettlebell swing. Ten of each. EMOM — every minute on the minute. Five rounds.

**[Bonesy attempts. Coach observes.]**

**COACH:** Squat down. Stand up. Drop the bell between your legs. Hinge. Snap your hips. Swing to chest height. Catch. Repeat. The flow trains your nervous system to transition under load. This is what athletic strength looks like. Not pretty. Effective.

---

## 🪝 RE-HOOK #4 (4:10)

**COACH:** One more thing. The mistake that *guarantees* you join me.

---

## ⚠️ COMMON MISTAKE + FIX (4:15–4:45)

**[Bonesy's knees collapse inward. Red X overlay. Slow-mo.]**

**COACH:** Knees caving. That's not a squat. That's a prayer to the ACL gods. They do not answer. Drive your knees *out* against the bar path. Push the floor apart. If you can't, the weight is too heavy. Drop it. Your ego is not heavier than your knees.

**BONESY:** What if I'm scared of the weight?

**COACH:** Then you are listening. Fear is data. Load less. Move better. Add it back when the form holds. Simple. Not easy.

---
