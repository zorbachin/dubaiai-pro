# Episode 01 — Voice Recording Sheet

## SETUP
- Quiet room. Soft furnishings around you for dampening.
- Mic 6 inches from mouth, slightly off-axis (avoid plosives).
- GarageBand → New audio track → arm for recording → 48kHz / 24-bit.
- Record one character all the way through first, then the other.
- Re-record any line that doesn't feel right. Disk is cheap.

---

## 🎙️ COACH SKULLY — DIRECTION

**Voice target:** Pavel Tsatsouline meets Sam Elliott meets a man who just buried his own dog.

- Drop your natural pitch ~1.5 semitones (we'll deepen further in post).
- Slow your cadence. Pause for half a beat after most periods.
- Drill-sergeant calm — never raise your volume, ever.
- Land each command word with a slight downward inflection: *"Brace."* not *"Brace?"*
- For sarcasm beats, do not change your tone. Keep it flat. The contrast does the work.

---

## 🎙️ BONESY — DIRECTION

**Voice target:** Michael Cera who is also actively having a panic attack.

- Raise your natural pitch ~1 semitone (we'll lift further in post).
- Slight stammers OK. Trail off OK. Audible breaths between thoughts OK.
- Every line should sound like you're not sure of the answer.
- For worried beats, almost-whisper them. For confused beats, drag out the vowels.

---

## 📋 RECORDING SCRIPT

Read these aloud in order. Numbers correspond to script beats — match the file naming.

```
[001 — COACH] (low, dry)
Ninety percent of you squat with your ego. The other ten percent are lying about the ego part.

[002 — BONESY] (small, worried)
Is that... normal?

[003 — COACH]
Before you load the bar. Before you even think about loading the bar. There is one cue. Skip it and the next four minutes will not save you. I'm dead. I have time. Do you?

[004 — COACH]
Goblet squat first. Always. Before you back-squat anything, you earn it with a goblet. Hold a kettlebell at your chest. Elbows tucked. Feet shoulder-width. Toes flared fifteen degrees. Not thirty. You are not auditioning for the Bolshoi.

[005 — BONESY]
What's a Bolshoi?

[006 — COACH]
A ballet company. Point your toes less.

[007 — BONESY]
Hands?

[008 — COACH]
Cup the horns of the bell. Squeeze. Tension is universal. Grip the bell, then glutes, then lats, then cross-tension through the spine. You are a coiled spring. Hardstyle, or no style.

[009 — BONESY]
Okay. Hardstyle. Got it. What about my back?

[010 — COACH]
Stacked. Neutral spine. Chest tall. Take a breath through your belt. Breathing behind the shield. Hold it.

[011 — COACH] (lean-in whisper, one eye lit)
Here is the truth no algorithm rewards. Your squat does not stall because your legs are weak. It stalls because your intent is weak. You descend like you're sitting in a chair. Don't. Descend like you're crushing the floor on the way down. Watch.

[012 — COACH]
Three seconds down. Hips back first — close that car door with your spine. Then knees track over toes. Sit between your heels. Not behind them. Not in front of them. Between.

[013 — BONESY] (strained)
Something cracked.

[014 — COACH]
That was the ego. Keep going. Hip crease below the kneecap. Anything shallower is a quarter squat, and a quarter squat is just an expensive way to stand up.

[015 — COACH] (voiceover, calm, almost meditative)
Drive your knees out as you descend. Not forward. Out. You are spreading the floor apart with the outside edges of your feet. This is the cue. Do it and the squat fixes itself. Schoenfeld twenty-ten on knee valgus and force production — read it or don't, the bar doesn't care.

[016 — COACH]
Now. Programming. This is where most lifters waste two years. Pay attention.

[017 — COACH]
Strength. Four to six sets. Three to five reps. Two to three minutes rest. Heavy load. Sub-maximal. Stop one rep before you want to. Strength is a skill, not a grind. You don't grind the piano. You practice it.

[018 — BONESY]
And for size?

[019 — COACH]
Hypertrophy. Three to four sets. Eight to twelve reps. Sixty to ninety seconds rest. Moderate load. The muscle should cry. The literature is settled — Schoenfeld, Krieger, every PhD with a barbell — moderate-load, moderate-rep maximizes growth per unit of effort.

[020 — BONESY]
What if I want to squat more often?

[021 — COACH]
Frequency. Twice, three times a week. Three working sets per session. Lower load. Grease the groove. The squat is technique-heavy. Frequency wins for technique-heavy lifts. The body learns through repetition, not violence.

[022 — BONESY]
And volume?

[023 — COACH]
Volume day. Once a week. Five to six working sets. Eat the suffering in one sitting. Only for the advanced. For everyone else: frequency.

[024 — BONESY]
Which do I pick?

[025 — COACH]
Whichever one you'll still be doing in sixteen weeks. Consistency beats theory. Theory is for skeletons.

[026 — BONESY]
...you are a skeleton.

[027 — COACH] (beat, then flat)
I know.

[028 — COACH]
One more thing. The flow. Once your goblet is honest, you earn this: goblet squat into a kettlebell swing. Ten of each. EMOM — every minute on the minute. Five rounds.

[029 — COACH]
Squat down. Stand up. Drop the bell between your legs. Hinge. Snap your hips. Swing to chest height. Catch. Repeat. The flow trains your nervous system to transition under load. This is what athletic strength looks like. Not pretty. Effective.

[030 — COACH]
One more thing. The mistake that guarantees you join me.

[031 — COACH]
Knees caving. That's not a squat. That's a prayer to the ACL gods. They do not answer. Drive your knees out against the bar path. Push the floor apart. If you can't, the weight is too heavy. Drop it. Your ego is not heavier than your knees.

[032 — BONESY]
What if I'm scared of the weight?

[033 — COACH]
Then you are listening. Fear is data. Load less. Move better. Add it back when the form holds. Simple. Not easy.

[034 — COACH]
Now go get it. Three sets. Sub-maximal. Stop one rep before you want to. Film the side angle. Watch the playback. Fix one thing. Come back tomorrow — bench press, same time — unless you'd rather end up like us.

[035 — BONESY]
I really don't recommend it.
```
