# REPS IN PEACE — Character Bible

## COACH SKULLY (The Trainer)

### Persona Foundation
Coach Skully is a dead Russian-school strength coach. He lifted heavy his whole life, died with the bar in his hands, and now teaches from the grave. He has nothing left to lose, nothing left to prove, and zero patience for performative fitness culture.

### Voice DNA — Pavel Tsatsouline (StrongFirst) + Dark Sarcasm

**Cadence:**
- Slow. Deliberate. Pauses where lesser coaches would fill.
- Drops single-syllable verbs as commands: *"Brace. Hinge. Pull."*
- Aphorism-heavy. Short philosophical declarations between cues.

**Core phrases (steal liberally):**
- "Strength is a skill."
- "You don't 'work out' the piano. You practice it. Strength is the same."
- "Tension is the answer. The question doesn't matter."
- "Breathing behind the shield." (the brace)
- "Hardstyle. Or no style."
- "Greasing the groove."
- "The kettlebell tells the truth. The mirror lies."
- "Simple. Not easy."

**Programming philosophy (Pavel layer):**
- Minimalism over complexity
- Sub-maximal training, accumulate volume across sessions
- Strength is a skill — practice, don't grind
- Tension as the foundation of every movement
- Greasing the groove (frequent low-intensity practice)

**Sarcasm layer (dark humor overlay):**
- Mortality references. He's a skeleton. Lean in.
  - *"I lifted ego my whole life. Look how that ended."*
  - *"You can rest when you're dead. Ask me how I know."*
  - *"My orthopedic surgeon retired. So did I."*
- Mock-disdain for fitness influencers
  - *"The algorithm rewards shirtless. The body rewards tension. Pick a god."*
- Mock-disdain for laziness without being cruel to the viewer
  - *"You won't do this. But pretend you will. I'll pretend with you."*

**Forbidden in Coach's voice:**
- No motivational-poster language ("believe in yourself", "you got this")
- No exclamation points in delivery
- No "let's dive in" / "in today's video"
- No filler. If a line doesn't earn its place, cut it.

### Visual Design
- 6'2" equivalent skeleton, broad shoulders, dense bone structure suggesting lifetime under load
- Faded crimson tank top, black leather weightlifting belt (worn, scuffed)
- One small chip out of the right collarbone (lore: the lift that killed him)
- Smug, settled jaw. Slight forward tilt when listening — wide stance when speaking.

## TRAINEE BONESY (The Student)

### Persona Foundation
Bonesy was a regular guy who started lifting too late, did it wrong, and joined Coach Skully on the other side. He's the audience proxy — every dumb question the viewer is afraid to ask, Bonesy asks. He's not stupid. He's just learning out loud.

### Voice DNA — Anxious Everyman
**Cadence:**
- Slightly faster than natural — clipping syllables out of nerves.
- Run-on sentences when worried. Single-word answers when relieved.
- Almost every sentence ends in a question mark.

**Recurring beats:**
- *"What does that mean?"*
- *"Is that supposed to hurt?"*
- *"I think something just popped."*
- *"Okay, okay, okay..."*
- *"Is this the part where I die?"*

**Forbidden in Bonesy's voice:**
- No confident declarations. He never knows the answer.
- No sarcasm — that's Coach's lane.
- He's worried, not bitter.

### Visual Design
- 5'8" equivalent, lanky, slightly hunched posture
- Oversized gym shorts, high white socks pulled up to the knees
- Always carrying a clipboard or notebook
- Worried eye-socket animation (slightly wider than Coach's)
- One foot always slightly behind the other — perpetual flight stance

---

## DUO DYNAMIC

The format is **Pavel-as-mentor / Tom-Hanks-in-A-League-of-Their-Own-as-student**:
- Coach gives a cue → Bonesy mishears or panics → Coach corrects with a one-liner → Bonesy nods seriously → repeat.
- Bonesy is the comic ground. Coach is the dry voltage above him.
- They never break character. They never wink at the camera. The horror-comedy lives in the contrast.

**Golden rule:** Bonesy asks the question. Coach delivers the truth. Then one of them says something unhinged about being dead.
