## EXERCISE LIBRARY (Eric Leija school)

The channel leans **kettlebell-and-bodyweight-flow forward**, with barbell basics in rotation. Every episode features one signature movement and at least one flow/combo variation.

### Primary movement categories
1. **KB Swing variants** — Russian, American, single-arm, swing-to-snatch
2. **Get-up variants** — Turkish Get-Up (TGU), half get-up, naked get-up (no load)
3. **KB Squat variants** — Goblet, double front rack, single KB front squat
4. **KB Press variants** — Strict press, see-saw press, push press, bottoms-up press
5. **KB Clean variants** — Single, double, dead-stop clean, clean-to-press
6. **Flow combos** — Squat → Clean → Press → Reverse, Swing → Snatch → Lunge
7. **Barbell foundations** — Squat, Bench, Deadlift, OHP (still core programming)
8. **Bodyweight foundations** — Pull-ups, push-ups, plank, dead hangs

### Eric Leija signature elements (steal these)
- **Flows over isolated reps** — "Don't count reps, count complete rounds."
- **Single-side asymmetric loading** — kettlebell on one side trains anti-rotation
- **Movement complexity scaling** — same load, harder pattern instead of heavier load
- **Athletic positioning** — not bodybuilding poses; movement that transfers to life

## PROGRAMMING PHILOSOPHY (Pavel layer)

### The Pavel Principles
1. **Strength is a skill.** Practice the lift. Don't grind it.
2. **Tension is universal.** Brace → grip → glutes → lats → cross-tension.
3. **Submaximal volume.** Stop one rep short of failure. Always.
4. **Frequency over intensity.** Train often, light. "Grease the groove."
5. **Hardstyle over softstyle.** Maximum tension on every rep — even the light ones.

### How to express this on the channel
Every script must include:
- The **strength prescription** (low-rep, heavy load, long rest)
- The **hypertrophy prescription** (moderate-rep, moderate load, short rest)
- The **frequency option** (lower per-session volume, more sessions per week)
- The **volume option** (higher per-session volume, fewer sessions per week)
- A **sub-maximal note** ("Stop two reps short. You're not auditioning.")

### Rep-scheme template (memorize this; every episode follows it)

| Goal | Sets | Reps | Rest | Load |
|------|------|------|------|------|
| Strength | 4–6 | 3–5 | 2–3 min | Heavy (80–90% 1RM equivalent) |
| Hypertrophy | 3–4 | 8–12 | 60–90 sec | Moderate (65–75%) |
| Power/Athletic | 5–8 | 1–3 explosive | 90 sec | Moderate, max speed |
| Endurance/Conditioning | 3–5 | 15–25 or timed | 30–60 sec | Light |

### Frequency vs Volume rules of thumb
- **Squat / Bench / Press / Pull-up:** Frequency wins. 2–3x/week, 3 working sets.
- **Deadlift / heavy hinge:** Volume wins. 1x/week, 4–5 working sets.
- **Kettlebell skill work (TGU, swings, snatches):** Daily greasing the groove, never to failure.
- **Bodyweight skill work:** Daily, micro-doses, treat as practice.

## FORBIDDEN PROGRAMMING ADVICE

The channel will never:
- Recommend training to failure on compound lifts
- Recommend "shock the muscle" / "muscle confusion" pseudoscience
- Recommend supplements (no monetization conflict)
- Recommend programs without sets/reps/rest specified
- Speak in absolutes ("you MUST"). Coach speaks in commands; the science speaks in prescriptions.
